Course Syllabus

CP363 - Database I

Physics & Computer Science - Wilfrid Laurier University

Course Information

Introduction to database systems. Topics include data models, query languages, database design, recovery and concurrency, integrity and security.

Pre-requisites: CP164 (or CP114)

Credit: 0.5

Hours per week: Lecture/Discussion: 3

Course Overview

Welcome to CP363 - Database I. This course offers a comprehensive introduction to database systems, with a strong emphasis on relational database concepts, design, and implementation. You will explore key topics including data models, query languages (with a focus on SQL), database design using Entity-Relationship modeling, and relational database theory.

Additionally, the course covers critical aspects of database management systems, including data integrity, security, and mechanisms for concurrency control and recovery.

Structured in a thematic and progressive manner, each module integrates theoretical knowledge with hands-on practice using industry-standard tools. This practical approach ensures you build both the conceptual foundation and applied skills needed for real-world database management and development roles.

Course Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course, students should be able to:

  • Explain the fundamental concepts of relational database systems and their importance in modern information systems
  • Design effective database schemas using Entity-Relationship modeling techniques
  • Implement database designs using SQL, including creating tables with appropriate constraints
  • Develop complex SQL queries to retrieve and manipulate data from multiple tables
  • Apply normalization techniques to improve database designs and eliminate redundancies
  • Analyze database performance issues and implement appropriate indexing strategies
  • Evaluate different database technologies and their suitability for specific application scenarios
  • Create database views and stored procedures to enhance database functionality
  • Demonstrate effective teamwork skills through collaborative database projects

These outcomes are progressively developed throughout the course, with early modules building foundational knowledge that is applied and expanded in later modules.

Online Learning Expectations

This course is delivered in an asynchronous online format, meaning there are no scheduled class times. This approach offers several benefits:

  • Self-Paced Learning: You can spend more time on challenging concepts and move quickly through familiar material
  • Flexibility: Access and review course materials (lecture recordings, slides, readings) weekly according to your own schedule
  • Work-Life Balance: Better accommodation for students with jobs, family responsibilities, or other commitments

Students are expected to:

  • Complete weekly learning activities according to the course schedule
  • Submit assignments and take quizzes by the specified due dates
  • Actively participate in online discussions through MyLearningSpace
  • Check course announcements regularly
  • Contact the instructor promptly with any questions or concerns
  • Take responsibility for managing time effectively and maintaining consistent progress

The course is designed to foster active learning through interactive activities, discussions, and practical exercises rather than passive consumption of content.

Assessment Information

You are required to pass the final exam in order to pass the course. The Department of Physics & Computer Science requires that students must write the final exam in person on the Waterloo campus (unless otherwise specified due to exceptional circumstances).

If you pass the final exam, the following evaluation is applied:

Assessment Weighting
Weekly Quizzes 10%
Assignments/project (6) 25%
Video Presentation 5%
Midterm Test (in-person) 25%
Final Exam (in-person) 35%
Total 100%

No tests or assignments are dropped from the course evaluation.

Weekly Quizzes

Each week includes a short online quiz (5-8 multiple-choice questions) testing your understanding of fundamental concepts from that week's material. Quizzes have a 10-minute time limit and must be completed within the week the material is posted.

Quiz Flexibility Policy: You may miss 2 quizzes without penalty, or if you complete all quizzes, your lowest 2 quiz scores will be dropped. This policy accommodates unexpected circumstances without requiring formal documentation.

Assignments and Projects

Six culminating assignments which are parts of the project are worth 25% of the final grade. Students will submit each assignment to Dropbox by the assigned deadline. Project is a team-based work; students should work within a team of a maximum of 2.

For team projects, each submission should include a brief statement outlining the contribution of each team member. Grading will consider both the overall quality of the work and individual contributions.

Video Presentation

Each student will prepare a 5-7 minute video presentation on an assigned database topic. Presentations will be shared with the class through a dedicated discussion board, and students are expected to view and comment on at least 2 other presentations. The presentation should include a clear explanation of the topic, relevant examples, and visual aids (slides, diagrams, etc.).

Grading will be based on clarity, content, engagement, and adherence to the time limit. Peer feedback will also be considered in the final grade.

Midterm Exam

The midterm exam will be conducted in person on campus, as per departmental recommendations. It will cover course material from Weeks 1 to 6 and may include multiple-choice questions, short answer questions, and hands-on SQL exercises.

The exam will be closed-book, and all students must adhere to the university’s academic integrity policies. Students must write the midterm during the scheduled time. No make-up exams will be provided unless there is a valid, documented reason approved by the instructor.

Final Exam

The final exam will cover all course material and will be held online. The exam format will be similar to the midterm exam, with a mix of multiple-choice questions, short answer questions, and practical SQL exercises. The final exam will be worth 35% of the final grade.

Students must complete the final exam within the scheduled time frame. No make-up exams will be offered unless there is a valid, documented reason accepted by the instructor.

Students are required to pass the final exam in order to pass the course. The final exam will be scheduled by the registrar's office, and students should check the course page on MyLearningSpace for updates.

Course Policies

Missed Assignments

An assignment not submitted by the due date will receive a zero unless a valid, documented reason is provided. If a documented reason is supplied and accepted by the instructor, the assignment must be submitted within one week of the original due date. A copy or the original of the documented reason must be provided to and approved by the instructor.

Missed Midterm

A missed midterm exam will receive a zero unless there is a valid, documented reason accepted by the instructor. The student must complete the makeup exam on a date scheduled by the instructor. Missing the makeup exam will also result in a zero.

An extenuating circumstance is defined as a rare and severe event over which a student has no control or ability to anticipate (e.g., a severe accident or an emergent medical condition).

Final Exam

To be announced by the registrar's office. If you are considering registering for a special examination or event, you should select a time outside the examination period. Consult Academic Regulations in the Academic Calendar for special circumstances for examination deferment.
